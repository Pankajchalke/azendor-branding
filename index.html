Outsourced Brand Management

Several clients have outsourced their brand management to Azendor. This gives them the advantage of getting very senior and experienced professionals to manage their brands at a fraction of the cost of getting similarly qualified people as full time employees. This service includes:

  • Defining the brand strategy.
  • Converting the brand strategy into creative briefs (Advertising, PR, Digital & Social Media, Event, Content etc.).
  • Supervising the execution agencies' work to ensure that it is aligned with the brand strategy.
  • Working with clients to choose and brief the execution agencies (Creative, Media, Design, Digital, PR, Content, Direct Marketing / CRM etc.), including managing the entire 'pitch' process.
  • Helping clients set up their internal brand management teams, including help with hiring and training the employees, if required.

Sourabh Mishra

Founder & Partner

Sourabh was the Chief Strategy Officer at Saatchi & Saatchi India, TBWA\India, Everest and Bates CHI & Partners India.

He also led the brand strategy planning function at Ogilvy (Mumbai and Bangalore offices), McCann Erickson (Bangalore office) and Contract (Bangalore and Delhi offices). His experience spans across categories and includes brands like Parle (Monaco, Hide N Seek), Reliance Capital, Cadbury (5 Star, Perk, Bournvita, Tang), Limca, Sprite, Coca Cola, SET MAX, ITC Cigarettes, TVS 2 Wheelers, Skoda Cars, Lenovo, Titan, Kwality Wall’s, BPL, OLX India, ITC Ashirvaad, Ayurvedic Concepts (Himalaya), Skoda etc.

His views on branding topics have been published in leading industry publications. He has been a speaker at several industry events and teaches marketing communications at business schools as guest faculty. He also mentors start-ups at AIC NMIMS and IEF.

Magic Bus

Going into the future, Magic Bus, as a non-profit organization had identified the individual donor segment as an important source of funding, moving away from its primary dependency on corporate donor segments.

Research by Azendor showed that the story to get individual donors interested needed to be different from the story that was being communicated to the corporate donors. It needed to be simpler, easy to comprehend and highly emotional in its appeal.

The process started with further research amongst the key internal stakeholders, beneficiaries and donors. This was followed by a day long workshop with the key leadership team at Magic Bus, moderated by Azendor, to arrive at the brand idea. The output from the workshop was converted into a formal brand strategy document, which was used by the Magic Bus team to brief its creative agencies for the new communication, targeting individual donors.
