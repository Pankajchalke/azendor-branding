Outsourced Brand Management

Some of our clients outsource their brand management to Azendor. This gives them the advantage of getting senior, experienced professionals to manage their brands at a fraction of the cost of getting similarly qualified people as full-time employees. This service includes:

  • Defining the brand strategy.
  • Converting the brand strategy into creative briefs (Advertising, PR, Digital & Social Media, Event, Content etc.).
  • Working with clients to choose and brief the execution agencies (Creative, Media, Design, Digital, PR, Content, Direct Marketing / CRM etc.), including managing the entire 'pitch' process.
  • Supervising the execution agencies' work to ensure that it is aligned with the brand strategy
  • Helping clients set up their internal brand management teams, including help with hiring and training the employees, if required.

Fractal Analytics

As a part of its overall strategy to achieve its very ambitious business growth objective, Fractal’s brief to us was to look at its branding and recommend changes, if any, to align its brand to its business objectives.

As a first step, the marketing task was arrived at, in a client-led joint workshop: to grow the business, grow the average value of each project, not just increase the number of projects.

We realised that in order to do so, Fractal had to engage its clients in conversations about the larger business issues they faced, and not just remain confined to specialist data-analytics led discussions.

Research among Fractal’s various stakeholders – customers, employees, advisors, investors, industry bodies – helped us to understand the brand perception of Fractal at that time, which could possibly be a barrier to the new kind of conversations Fractal wanted to have with its clients.

A day-long workshop, moderated by Azendor, with the Fractal leadership team, pointed towards the new direction which would help achieve the marketing task. This direction was taken forward into a formal brand strategy, and was used to brief creative and design agencies for a new brand identity.

We worked with Fractal’s team in the creative pitch process to finalise an agency and were also associated with the team in the process of finalising a new brand identity.

Magic Bus

Going into the future, Magic Bus, as a non-profit organization had identified the individual donor segment as an important source of funding, moving away from its primary dependency on corporate donor segments.

Research by Azendor showed that the story to get individual donors interested needed to be different from the story that was being communicated to the corporate donors. It needed to be simpler, easy to comprehend and highly emotional in its appeal.

The process started with further research amongst the key internal stakeholders, beneficiaries and donors. This was followed by a day long workshop with the key leadership team at Magic Bus, moderated by Azendor, to arrive at the brand idea. The output from the workshop was converted into a formal brand strategy document, which was used by the Magic Bus team to brief its creative agencies for the new communication, targeting individual donors.

Stellar Value Chain Solutions

In 2016, Mr. Anshuman Singh had shared an audacious dream with Azendor as his brand brief: to create a new company in the logistics / supply chain domain that will redefine “managing supply chain / logistics” to “adding value by creating value chain transformation” for its client organisations. In the process, improve India’s rank in the global LPI (Logistics Performance Index).

This resulted in a six-month project, which included formally defining the brand strategy, creating a brand brief, running the creative agency pitch process, selecting an agency, creating the final brand identity with the chosen agency, working on the initial communications plan, hiring and training a brand communications manager, and ensuring that the brand management process was seamlessly integrated within Stellar.

This was Azendor’s first B-O-T brand project (build – operate – transfer, or outsourced brand management) – a model that many clients are finding very interesting to explore.

Roshi

The exercise led to the thought of 'good health made pleasurable'. We worked with the client and the design agency in the creative development process leading to finalising the name and the brand identity for Roshi.

La Folie

La Folie already had its brand identity in place but needed a brand idea to take forward its communications campaign. We worked with the client and their communications partners to craft a brand purpose for La Folie - rooted in the product category of premium chocolate products as well as the core values that the founders believed in. Drawing inspiration from the 'slow movement', this was articulated as 'slow things down so that people can meaningfully connect with the truly important things in their lives'.

Beco

The task was to launch a new range of sustainable and eco-friendly everyday use products like paper tissues, kitchen towels etc., targeted at millennials, who are getting increasingly more conscious about how their consumption choices impact the environment. We worked with the client team and the design agency to co-create the core thought for the brand - helping people ‘responsibly enjoy a good lifestyle’ through sustainable products. We worked with the creative agency to help develop the brand name - Beco - and its identity.
